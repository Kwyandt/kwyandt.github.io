CSCE 572: Human-Computer Interactions

This course focuses on two aspects of human-computer interaction, the technical side of research and analysis, and the application side of design and development. In addition to exams and quizzes, this class has a semester-long group project requiring us to research, design, and test a product concept.


Role

Designer, Researcher

Tools

Figma, Google Forms

Company

CSCE 572 (Harvey, Soraya, Jackie, Gabriel, Kat)

Overview

As a team we present Tindify: an app for music discovery through a basic online 'dating' UI concept. The purpose of this app is to help users discover music based on their music preferences, pulling from the spotify API onto the mobile app. It will show users new music, let them sort through the music easily, and export curated playlists to the music listening platform of their choosing. Local artists can create an artist profile to have their music highlighted on the platform to people in their area.

Process

Low Fidelity Prototype

After defining the mobile app concept, our team brainstormed features that were the core function of the app, and additional features that were reach goals. We developed two designs of the app with these core functions in mind.


Design Iteration I

These lo-fi prototypes were presented to the class and discussed, and design 1 was decided to move to high fidelity prototyping with aspects of design 2. We developed a high fidelity protoype with semi-complete interactive features to be used in a cognitive walkthrough. Click here to access design iteration 1.


Design Iteration II

(In Progress) Based on class lecture and guidance from our professor, we designed to evaluation methods, a cognitive walkthrough with our high fidelity prototype and a survey to test our core features. This feedback will be used to guide the next design iteration.
