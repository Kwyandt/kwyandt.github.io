ANTH 161: The Study of Human Origins

The final project for this couse was an unessay, anything that can explain your topic of choice and educate the listener in a non-tradition essay format. I researched the evidence of diseased in human evolution, and decided to present my work using an interactive website, remniscint of websites from the early 2000s. This combines my interest in educational technology design and my interests in research.

Overview

This information is delived in 4 key sections: a brief history to serve as the introduction, the evidence of disease serving as the main body text, in interactive hominin phylogeny to inform the viewer, and a "fun facts" section to allow the user to actively explore and learn on the website. While many of the project in this course focused on one species, this project encapsulates many species and time periods, which is why the phylogeny was so important. I encourage you to explore the website here.

Process

Research

This was a lecture based class which provided us with much of the background knowledge needed to understand the research in the field, however for this project we dug deeper into the field for academic journals and non-biased articles. We completed a miniature annotated bibliogrpahy to summarize our findings.


Card Sorting

After synthesizing the information, my next task was organizing the information in a fun, logical flow so that users of all demographics could understand. I used a card sorting method to determine the categories that are shown to the right.


Interactive Design

I completed the design using interactive Figma components. This was my first use of Figma prototyping and I learned how to create high-fidelity mock-ups that are typically used in cognitive walkthroughs. This design underwent multiple design iterations with feedback from my teacher to ensure high quality research, and from co-workers to ensure high quality design.
